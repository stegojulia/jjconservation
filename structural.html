Hand lining of an oversized painting

This large format (2292 x 1526mm) painting suffered from multiple structural issues, including insufficient tension, sagging, tears and deterioration of tacking margins, which required full lining. Due to its size, the treatment could not be carried out on the lining table. The canvas was tensioned with the method of pre-stretching - attachment of the support to the workspace with Kraft paper and gum tape. Two conservators then worked together to symmetrically attach the canvas to the BEVA-coated lining sailcloth with hand irons.

  • Excellent communication skills were essential to complete the project. Due to the size, we had to work as a team in a co-ordinated manner at all stages of the treatment.
  • The project provided me with experience in planning of studio activities to carry out a complex treatment.
  • Hand lining and pre-stretching were an opportunity to practice hand-skills in time-sensitive tasks.
