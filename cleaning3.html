Removal of thick varnish on a lean paint layer

The varnish on this painting was very degraded and unevenly applied, with a very thick application in some places. Varnish removal was carried out with several free solvent mixtures, which were adjusted depending on the paint sensitivity in various areas.

The paint in the foreground (sea area) had a blanched appearance and appeared very lean, which is possibly why it had not been thoroughly cleaned in the past. The thick layer of varnish required several applications of solvent. The yellowed varnish was removed but a thin layer of the coating was left on the surface to protect the sensitive paint layers. Small swabs with acetone were used to clean around the faint brown rigging and sensitive red paint on the flags. Acetone was used afterwards to remove final residues. An isolating varnish was applied to the front of the painting by brush, which helped to even out any differences in saturation.
