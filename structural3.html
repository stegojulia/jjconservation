Consolidation & thread-to-thread tear mending

There were three tears present in the primary support. The multidirectional tear had been stabilised with pressure-sensitive tape before the painting entered the studio. Although not an idea solution, the tape had prevented excessive distortion to the damaged area, and was left in place until right before repair to limit mended. It was then carefully removed with tweezers, with the help of solvent to reduce the mechanical impact on the canvas. One of the tears was located just behind the strainer bar. A decision was made to repair the tear without removing the canvas from its strainer, having considered the time constraint, the brittleness of the degraded canvas and the limited planar distortion. Instead, a simple PlastazoteÂ® construction with a slightly sloped base was constructed to improve the visual access to the area during the repair. The tears were repaired with the thread-to-thread technique. Bridging was employed for threads too short to be joined and to effectively correct the distortion present. Ironing tack was used, applied to the end of the dental tool, to ensure fast curing. Neat joining of threads presented some challenge due to very fine canvas support. Skills developed included:

  • Thread-to-thread tear repair.
  • Problem solving: limted visual access to one of the tears.
  • Decision making: considering the time available and general condition of the painting to choose the best course of action.
