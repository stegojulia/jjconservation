Methodological removal of two varnish layers

Two degraded coatings were found on the surface of this painting: a slightly darkened top layer of modern synthetic varnish and some of the thick patchy remnants of this natural resin varnish. The two layers were removed in stages. The treatment revealed some brilliant colours which had been completely obscured before treatment. The varnish and over-paint removal also revealed the extent of paint layer abrasion, caused by historic injudicious cleaning methods and drying cracks particularly in the foreground area of sea.

Some problematic areas include red and green details which displayed some sensitivity to solvent. To reduce their exposure to solvents, small, almost dry swabs of solvent were used to gently draw out the varnish. Small swabs were also used to carefully remove varnish residues surrounding the signature in the bottom left corner. Particular care was taken when cleaning the cloud of smoke, which was one of the areas which had not been cleaned in the past and was covered with a very discoloured layer of aged resin. As a result, it was difficult to see where the smoke originally ended, and consequently whether any damage was being caused by cleaning. The area was carefully examined under magnification and with the use of a UV torch before and during treatment.
