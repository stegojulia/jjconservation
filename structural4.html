Split repairs for a panel painting

The objective of this project was to address the five splits present in the panel support. Previous repair, attempted by attaching rectangular blocks of wood to the reverse of the panel, had failed and the splits were misaligned. This treatment was carried out after the painting had been cleaned. The reverse had also been treated for mould.

The structural treatment aimed at removing the rectangular blocks and old adhesive used in previous interventions to facilitate the retreatment of the panel to prepare the panel for re-alignment and re-joining of the splits.

Splits were repaired one by one according to the following methodology: a ‘dry run’ of the re-joining procedure was then carried out, using weights to realign the splits and clamps to bring the edges together. The exact position of the weights and clamps was recorded with chalk and photographs. The adhesive was applied to the inside of the split and the weights and clamps were repositioned. Each split was then left to set, clamped and weighted, for 24 hours.
