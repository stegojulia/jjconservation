In-painting of losses and abrasion in an oversized painting

An isolating varnish was applied to the front of the painting by brush. Visual reintegration included filling using Champagne Chalk and retouching with dry pigments. This included in-painting of the filled losses and glazing on some of the most abraded areas. In particular, the areas of discolouration related to the stretcher bars required reintegration. Due to the size of the painting and the large area of damage, this step of the treatment was carried out by three conservators. This required good communication to ensure the same level and method of reintegration in all areas. Finally, the painting was spray varnished.
