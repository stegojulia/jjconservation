Gelled solvent for poorly soluble varnish

The varnish layers were found to be very insoluble and extensive testing of cleaning agents and techniques was required to complete the treatment. The most effective way to remove the very thick discoloured varnish layers proved to be a gelled solvent at applied by brush and cleared using free solvent and cotton wool swabs. The solubility of the varnish and susceptibility to blanching varied throughout the painting, and so the technique of cleaning (action time of gel, size of area treated) had to be adjusted depending on the area. Light scraping with a scalpel was necessary in some places to remove residues softened by the gel.

The very distorted, damaged areas (such as the area around the previously repaired tear) required working in very small sections, working with small swabs around the distortions and losses. the centre of the upper and lower edges there were relatively small areas of extremely fragile paint that would delaminate during cleaning regardless of the method of varnish removal attempted. It was decided that the varnish would be lightly thinned and retouched at later stage to avoid further paint loss.
