Overpaint on a flaky portrait

The thickly applied natural resin varnish was removed with free solvents. Solubility varied depending on the location and thickness of the coating.

A significant amount of overpaint was found on the face of the sitter which suffered from paint loss and delamination. Technical examination suggested that a significant amount of original paint can be uncovered if the overpaint was removed. The area was consolidated first and the treatment was carried out with extreme care. Some overpaint was easily soluble with solvent. As much as possible was removed with very small swabs, working from the edges of the area inwards to avoid disturbing the paint. Other areas however, which had an appearance of a waxy fill, would only swell upon contact with solvent. When swollen, the overpaint briefly lost the cohesion with the original surface and was gently peeled off with tweezers and scalpel under magnification. Consolidation was carried out at this stage as needed.

Examination under magnification revealed that the area had previously been consolidated, and flakes of original paint were stacked on top of overpaint or fill. Removal of overpaint/fill there would have likely meant damaging the original material underneath, which was deemed unethical.

The overpaint found in the background was removed where it was deemed safe, and thinned in other areas.
