Technical examination to inform treatment

In this project I was able to use imaging techniques and cross-section analysis to document and facilitate decision making during conservation treatment. Selecting the right examination techniques to clarify any questions was key.

Cross-section analysis was used to establish presence of the top layer of paint in the cracks of the painting to supplement examination under the microscope. This technique was especially useful when examining the condition of the face of the sitter. Infrared reflectography (IR) was used to clarify the extent and location of overpaint in the background. The imaging results suggested two campaigns of retouching, likely using a carbon-based pigment.

X-radiography results were encouraging in terms of likely presence of a significant amount of original material under the layers of filling and retouching. This was useful to deciding whether overpaint removal presented warranted the risks involved in removal of the added material.

UV-induced fluorescence was used continuously throughout the treatment to assess the thickness and location of deteriorated varnish and so guide the varnish removal process.
