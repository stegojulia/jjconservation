Removal of varnish and insoluble overpaint

Removal of discoloured varnish and overpaint were addressed at this stage of the treatment.

The varnish was removed with free solvent on cotton swabs. The sensitive details were particularly carefully cleaned with thin, almost dry cotton swabs of acetone. The solubility parameters of the overpaint varied significantly throughout the painting. Some of the overpaint was removed at the varnish removal stage. Several solvent mixtures were used to clear the rest of the overpaint. Where especially poorly soluble, solvent was used to swell and mechanically remove overpaint and filling material which had been liberally applied over the areas of paint loss as well as the surrounding paint. The treatment revealed patchy discolouration underneath some overpaint and pressure-sensitive tape, possibly a result of a darkened oiling-out area. It could not be removed with solvents and stronger surface cleaning solutions. The best course of action seemed to be to reduce the appearance of the stains by sensitive retouching as not to put the paint layers under further stress with more testing.

The treatment successfully revealed a significant amount of original material. Although some areas of the painting were in a poor condition, some details like remaining sections of a signature were uncovered and documented.
